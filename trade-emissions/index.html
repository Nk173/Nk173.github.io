EMISSIONS EMBODIED IN INTERNATIONAL TRADE · 2022 · OECD 2025 EDITION

How trade redistributes emissions between economies

An illustrative guide to the trade-value and embodied-emissions networks used to identify unequal environmental burdens in global production.

HOW TO READ THE NETWORK

Trade value and embodied emissions describe the same flow differently

The upper network represents monetary trade; the lower network represents the emissions embodied in those transactions. A link becomes inequitable when its normalized emissions share is greater than its normalized trade-value share.

Emissions share5%÷Trade share2%=Inequity2.5×

The resulting inequitable-emissions network, GEI, keeps only directed links above 1×. These retained links determine the trophic hierarchy shown in the interactive network.
