I am a mathematician and data scientist working predominantly in the field of Computational Social Science with a PhD from Imperial College London and close to a decade of experience across academia, public policy, and applied research. Research

I hold a PhD from the Networks and Complexity Group at Imperial College London, with close to a decade of experience working at the intersection of complexity science, finance, and public policy. My research employs network science, statistical physics, dynamical systems, and agent-based modelling to investigate global production and trade networks — particularly diffusion, contagion, and how macroeconomic patterns arise from micro-level interactions. I have published in peer-reviewed journals including Royal Society Open Science, npj Clean Air, Springer Knowledge and Information Systems, and Economic and Political Weekly.

Previously, I was a Researcher with the Public Policy Programme at the Alan Turing Institute, where I developed an agent-based simulation framework for global production networks and built calibration workflows to match models to empirical data.

Policy & Applied Work

Before my PhD I spent close to a decade in applied policy research at Dvara Research (formerly IFMR Finance Foundation), where I founded and led the Social Protection Initiative — building the evidence base on last-mile exclusion in India's government-to-citizen benefits and payments. My work spanned financial inclusion, household finance, and welfare delivery: from the State of Exclusion study of cash-transfer delivery to impact evaluations of national programmes such as the Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana and the NPS-Swavalamban pension scheme.

I have advised and made submissions to regulators and government bodies — including the Reserve Bank of India, SEBI (the Working Group on the Social Stock Exchange), MeitY (on data-protection enforcement and national digital infrastructure), and the Ministry of Finance — and I was part of the team that won the World Bank Group's Mission Billion Challenge with Gram Vaani for reducing exclusions in digital-ID-based welfare. I also write for the national press, including The Hindu, India Development Review, and BusinessLine.

Building & Community

At Dvara I built the Dvara Open Online Repository for household finance, establishing research partnerships with IIT Madras, the Indian School of Business, IIM Ahmedabad, and the Stanford Centre on Global Poverty and Development, among others. I also co-founded the Madras Complexity Collective, a science-communication platform exploring complexity and emergence across language, transport, urban planning, ecology, and cognition.
