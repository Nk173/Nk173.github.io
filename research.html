Royal Society Open Science 13, 251246 · 2026 Journal Article
Tracing inequitable emissions in global trade using a networks approach
Nishanth Kumar, Henrik Jeldtoft Jensen & Eduardo Viegas
A network-science approach to tracing the inequity embodied in the emissions of international trade. Treating global trade as a directed network, the paper attributes emissions across producing and consuming countries and quantifies the structural inequity in how emissions are distributed relative to the gains from trade.

Economic Trading Susceptibility: Constructing Networks of Mutual Influence through the Fitness of Countries
Nishanth Kumar, Henrik Jeldtoft Jensen & Eduardo Viegas
Builds networks of mutual economic influence between countries from their trade "fitness", capturing how the emergence of economic blocks and the leverage nations exert on one another shape the globally interconnected economy — and how susceptible each country is to others through these directed influence networks.

Preprint · arXiv:2003.09715 2020 Preprint
Network structure of COVID-19 spread and the lacuna in India's testing strategy
Anand Sahasranaman & Nishanth Kumar
Characterises the network of early COVID-19 spread in India — estimating a transmission rate of 0.43 with growth driven by imported cases — and argues that a testing strategy limited to travellers and their contacts yields a biased picture that risks missing community transmission.

npj Clean Air 2026 Journal Article
Effects of urbanization, scale, and geography on air pollution in India
Anand Sahasranaman, Nishanth Kumar, Thilo Erbertseder, Michael Wurm, Hannes Taubenböck & Luís M. A. Bettencourt
Combines satellite and ground-level air-quality data to map NO₂ and PM₁₀ pollution across India's districts and cities (2005–19). Urbanization has an inverse relationship with NO₂ growth, yet the most urbanized districts carry the highest burden and those at intermediate urbanization (30–40%) see the largest increases — with the Indo-Gangetic Plain of particular concern.

Preprint · arXiv:2410.04737 2024 Preprint
Urbanization, economic development, and income distribution dynamics in India
Anand Sahasranaman, Nishanth Kumar & Luís M. A. Bettencourt
A systematic, internationally-comparable analysis of how India's economic and human development and its personal income distribution evolve as a function of urbanization — finding that India outperforms most nations on several development indicators as its cities grow, even as distributional challenges persist.

Economic & Political Weekly 58(25–26), 37–46 · 2023 Journal Article
Distributional dynamics of income in Indian states: Inequality, reallocation, and income transitions
Anand Sahasranaman & Nishanth Kumar
Studies the distribution of income across Indian states (2014–19), finding wide heterogeneity in inequality. In high-inequality states the income shares and real growth of the bottom decile have declined, and a stochastic income-growth model reveals a net redistribution of resources from poor to rich.

Preprint · arXiv:2010.03602 2020 Preprint
Income distribution and inequality in India: 2014–19
Anand Sahasranaman & Nishanth Kumar
Examines the evolution of income in India (2014–19): while aggregate inequality is broadly stable, the bottom of the distribution suffers large losses — the bottom ventile's income share falls ~38% with negative real growth — and the rural and urban bottoms diverge sharply.
